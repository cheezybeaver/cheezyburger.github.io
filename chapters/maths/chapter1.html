CHEMICAL REACTIONS

Amines behave as nucleophiles.

1. Basic Characters of Amines:

They are basic in nature and react with acids to form salts.

Amino salts are soluble in H2O and not in organic solvents. The salt is a base and can be converted back to the amine.

This reaction is the basis for the separation of amines from non-basic compounds insoluble in water.

pKb of ammonia = 4.75. Aliphatic amines are strong bases due to +I effect. Aromatic amines have a higher pKb than ammonia.

DIAZONIUM SALTS

Preparation:

The conversion of primary aromatic amines into diazonium salts is known as diazotisation.

Physical Properties:

Benzenediazonium chloride is a colorless crystalline solid. It is readily soluble, stable in cold but decomposes easily in a dry state.

Benzenediazonium fluoroborate is water-soluble and stable at room temperature.

Chemical Reactions:

A. Reactions involving displacement of nitrogen:

  1. Replacement by halide or cyanide ion (Sandmeyer's reaction).
  2. Replacement by iodide ion.
  3. Replacement by fluoride ion.
  4. Replacement by hydrogen.
  5. Replacement by hydroxyl group.
  6. Replacement by NO2 group.

B. Reactions involving retention of diazo group:

Coupling reaction.

TESTS

1. Carbylamine Reaction / Isocyanide Test:

Differentiates 1° amine from 2° and 3° amines.

2. Reaction with Nitrous Acid:

Differentiates between aliphatic and aromatic amines.

3. Hinsberg Reagent Test:

Separates 1°, 2°, and 3° amines.
