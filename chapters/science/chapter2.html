Introduction

Microorganisms, or microbes, are tiny living organisms that cannot be seen with the naked eye. They are found everywhere‚Äîin the air, water, soil, and even inside our bodies. While some microorganisms are beneficial, others can cause diseases.

Types of Microorganisms

1. Bacteria

Bacteria are single-celled organisms that can survive in diverse environments. They are used in making curd, antibiotics, and nitrogen fixation. However, some bacteria cause diseases like tuberculosis and cholera.

2. Fungi

Fungi include organisms like yeast and molds. Yeast is used in baking bread, while molds like Penicillium are used to produce antibiotics. Some fungi can spoil food and cause infections.

3. Protozoa

Protozoa are single-celled organisms that cause diseases like malaria and dysentery. They are often transmitted through contaminated water or insect bites.

4. Viruses

Viruses are non-living outside host cells but can cause diseases like cold, flu, and COVID-19. They reproduce only inside the cells of a host organism.

Food Preservation

1. Chemical Methods

Using salt, sugar, oil, or vinegar to prevent microbial growth.

2. Heat Treatment

Boiling milk or pasteurization to kill harmful microbes.

3. Cold Storage

Refrigeration slows down microbial activity.

Quick Quiz

Q1. Why are antibiotics ineffective against viruses?

Antibiotics target bacterial cell structures, but viruses lack these structures and reproduce inside host cells.
